FIELDS TO EXTRACT:

# Infectious Disease Model Extraction Fields

## Model Characteristics

**model_type**
The computational/mathematical framework used.
- agent-based, individual-based
- SEIR compartmental, SIR compartmental, SEIRS compartmental
- network, metapopulation, branching process
- statistical/regression, machine learning
- hybrid (compartmental + agent-based), microsimulation

**model_determinism**
Whether the model incorporates randomness.
- deterministic | stochastic | both | unspecified

Agent-based models are typically stochastic; ODE-based compartmental models are typically deterministic.

---

## Pathogen & Disease

**pathogen_name**
Scientific or common name of the causative agent.
- SARS-CoV-2, Influenza A(H1N1), Influenza A(H5N1)
- Plasmodium falciparum, Mycobacterium tuberculosis, Vibrio cholerae
- Dengue virus (DENV), Ebola virus (EBOV), HIV-1, RSV

**pathogen_type**
Biological classification.
- virus | bacterium | parasite | fungus | prion | unspecified

Common mappings:
- *Viruses:* COVID-19, influenza, measles, HIV, Ebola, dengue, Zika, RSV
- *Bacteria:* tuberculosis, cholera, plague, typhoid, pertussis
- *Parasites:* malaria, leishmaniasis, Chagas disease

**disease_name**
Clinical name of the disease (may differ from pathogen).
- COVID-19, influenza, malaria, tuberculosis, Ebola virus disease, dengue fever, AIDS, measles, cholera

---

## Population & Setting

**host_species**
The species affected or modeled.
- human, cattle, poultry, swine, wild birds
- mosquito vector, Aedes aegypti vector
- multi-host, human-animal interface

**primary_population**
The main human population subgroup studied.
- general population, general adult population
- children under 5, school-aged children (5-17), elderly (65+)
- healthcare workers, pregnant women
- men who have sex with men (MSM), people who inject drugs (PWID)
- immunocompromised individuals, hospitalized patients, travelers

**population_setting_type**
The physical/social context of transmission.
- community, household, hospital, long-term care facility
- school, university, workplace, prison
- refugee camp, cruise ship, military base
- urban, rural, mixed settings

---

## Geography & Time

**geographic_scope**
The spatial scale of the study.
- local/city-level | sub-national/regional | national | multi-country | continental | global | theoretical

**geographic_units**
Specific locations named in the study.
- Use "unspecified" if no location is named
- Examples: New York City, Lombardy, Wuhan, Sierra Leone, European Union, Sub-Saharan Africa

**study_dates_start** / **study_dates_end**
Beginning and end of the study period.
- Format: YYYY-MM-DD, YYYY-MM, or YYYY

**historical_vs_hypothetical**
Whether the study models past events or future scenarios.
- historical | hypothetical future outbreak | mixed | unspecified

---

## Study Purpose

**study_goal_category**
The primary analytical objective (select all that apply).
- forecast/nowcast — predicting future or current disease burden
- scenario analysis — comparing "what if" scenarios
- intervention evaluation — assessing effectiveness of control measures
- parameter estimation — estimating R₀, serial interval, etc.
- methodological — developing or comparing modeling methods
- transmission dynamics/drivers — understanding factors driving spread
- burden estimation — estimating total disease burden
- risk assessment — evaluating outbreak or importation risk

## Interventions

**intervention_present**
- yes | no | unspecified

**intervention_types** *(if present)*
- vaccination
- school closure, workplace closure
- testing and isolation, contact tracing
- social distancing, mask mandates
- antiviral treatment, prophylaxis
- vector control, insecticide-treated nets
- quarantine, travel restrictions
- hand hygiene, environmental cleaning

## Data & Methods

**data_used**
Types of data used to inform or validate the model.
- surveillance case data, line list data
- hospital admissions, ICU admissions
- mortality data, excess deaths
- serological survey data
- genomic/phylogenetic data
- mobility data, contact survey data
- wastewater surveillance, syndromic surveillance
- no data (theoretical model)

**data_source**
Specific repositories or datasets named.
- Johns Hopkins CSSE, WHO Global Health Observatory, CDC FluView
- GISAID, UK ONS, ECDC Surveillance Atlas
- Project Tycho, HealthMap, local health department
- Use "unspecified" if not named

**calibration_mentioned**
- yes | no

**calibration_techniques**
Statistical methods for parameter estimation or model fitting.
- maximum likelihood estimation (MLE)
- Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)
- approximate Bayesian computation (ABC)
- particle filtering / sequential Monte Carlo
- least squares optimization
- Bayesian inference
- ensemble Kalman filter, genetic algorithm

## Outcomes & Outputs

**key_outcome_measures**
Primary model outputs or metrics reported.
- incidence, cumulative cases
- hospitalizations, ICU admissions
- deaths, mortality rate
- R₀, effective reproduction number (Rₜ)
- attack rate, infection fatality rate (IFR)
- serial interval, generation time
- bed occupancy, healthcare demand
- years of life lost (YLL), DALYs
- probability of outbreak, epidemic threshold
- time to peak, epidemic duration
