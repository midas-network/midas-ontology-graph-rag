AVAILABLE ONTOLOGIES (select from these only):
ncbi_taxonomy: A comprehensive hierarchical classification system for all organisms, from bacteria and viruses to plants and animals, maintained by the National Center for Biotechnology Information. It provides unique taxonomic identifiers and standardized naming for millions of species used across biological databases worldwide.
iso_3166: An international standard published by the International Organization for Standardization that defines codes for countries, dependent territories, and special areas of geographical interest. It includes two-letter (alpha-2), three-letter (alpha-3), and numeric codes that are widely used in international trade, banking, and data systems.
geonames: A free geographical database containing over 11 million place names covering all countries, including cities, villages, administrative divisions, and natural features. It provides coordinates, population data, and administrative hierarchy information accessible through web services and downloadable data files.
apollo_sv: A standardized vocabulary developed for computational epidemic modeling that defines terms for diseases, populations, interventions, and model parameters. It enables interoperability between different epidemic simulation software by providing common definitions and data exchange formats.
midas_data: A metadata standard developed by the MIDAS (Models of Infectious Disease Agent Study) Network to facilitate sharing and reuse of infectious disease modeling data. It provides structured vocabularies for describing datasets, model inputs/outputs, and epidemiological parameters used in computational modeling.
doid: The Disease Ontology is a standardized ontology that provides consistent, reusable descriptions of human disease terms and their relationships. It integrates disease and medical vocabularies through cross-mapping to ICD, MeSH, and other clinical terminologies for use in biomedical research.
ido: The Infectious Disease Ontology provides formal definitions for entities and processes involved in infectious diseases, including pathogen transmission, host responses, and disease progression. It serves as a foundation for representing infectious disease knowledge in biomedical databases and clinical applications.
obi: The Ontology for Biomedical Investigations describes scientific investigations, including study designs, protocols, instrumentation, and data generated from experiments. It supports consistent annotation of research methodologies across diverse biomedical domains from genomics to clinical trials.
vo: The Vaccine Ontology is a community-based ontology that standardizes vaccine-related terms including vaccine components, immunization schedules, and immune responses. It supports vaccine data integration, literature mining, and computational analysis of vaccine efficacy and safety data.
stato: The Statistics Ontology provides standardized terms for describing statistical methods, tests, and parameters used in scientific research. It covers concepts from experimental design through data analysis, enabling consistent annotation of statistical approaches across studies and domains.

When you recommend apollo_sv, also recommend midas_data as well.
When you recommend doid, also recommend ido as well.
When you recommend obi, also recommend stato as well.
